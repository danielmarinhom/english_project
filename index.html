SIMPLE AND COMPOUND INTEREST

  • Simple and compound interest adjust values in financial transactions. Simple interest is corrected each period based on the initial amount
  • Compound interest, also known as interest on interest, increases the final value more over long periods due to continuous correction.

J = C * i * t
J: interest
C: initial amount (principal)
i: interest rate
t: transaction period


The total amount to be redeemed or paid off at the end of a period, called the total amount, is:
M = C + J

For compound interest, the formula is:
M = C * (1 + i) ^ t
M: total amount
C: principal
i: interest rate
t: period of time
